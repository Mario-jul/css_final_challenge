Corcovado National Park

Corcovado National Park

Chile also has its own Corcovado! It's called Corcovado National Park, located in the Los Lagos region, in the commune of Chaitén. This national park is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna, as well as pristine territories like the Gulf of Corcovado and Tic Toc Bay, along with the magnificent Corcovado or Puntiagudo volcano, a favorite among climbers..

Fray Jorge National Park

Nacional Corcovado National Park

Located in the Coquimbo region, Fray Jorge National Park is a humid forest situated beside the Atacama Desert, considered the driest desert in the world. A true oasis in the desert! Due to this unique characteristic, it is one of the most magical and mysterious national parks in the country. Through its trails, you can explore relict forests with over 440 tree species and capture unique photographs with maybe a culpeo fox or peregrine falcon as your protagonists.

Huerquehue National Park

Huerquehue National Park

This is one of those national parks that will steal your heart with its araucaria forests, eternal snows, and hidden lakes nestled amidst mountains. Here, silence reigns supreme, offering a true escape. Located in the Araucanía region, Huerquehue National Park is a paradise for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. Enjoy biking, trekking, camping, and exploring the stunning Verde and Tinquilco lakes, as well as the enchanting El Toro lagoon. Immerse yourself in the park's diverse flora and fauna along its many trails.

La Campana National Park

La Campana National Park

Designated as a protected area by the State, La Campana National Park in the Valparaíso region is divided into sectors: Granizo, Cajón Grande, and Palmas de Ocoa. The latter is the most striking part of the park as it is home to the largest number of Chilean palm trees, an endemic species considered the southernmost palm in the world. This national park boasts a great variety of flora and fauna from different parts of the country, including culpeo foxes, chinchillas, partridges, hawks, owls, and quails, which is why it is considered a World Biosphere Reserve.

Lauca National Park

Lauca National Park

Spanning 137,883 hectares, Lauca National Park in Arica is a natural wonder waiting to be discovered. Home to over 130 bird species, including the graceful vicuña, curious vizcacha, and majestic condor, this biosphere reserve offers an unforgettable adventure. Explore the charming villages of Parinacota and Chucuyo, camp beneath the shadow of the towering Parinacota and Pomerape volcanoes, or simply relax by the serene Lake Chungará. With opportunities for hiking, mountaineering, and wildlife observation, Lauca National Park promises an experience that will leave you in awe.

Pan de Azucar National Park

Pan de Azucar National Park

Located in the Antofagasta region, 30 km from the town of Chañaral, Pan de Azúcar National Park is a place where you can enjoy both nature and the sea. This national park is unique in having two beaches within its boundaries: Playa Blanca and Playa Los Piqueros, making it an oasis in the northern desert. Another highlight of this park is the opportunity to visit the Humboldt penguin colony on Pan de Azúcar Island.

Rapa Nui National Park

Rapa Nui National Park

Declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Rapa Nui National Park, located in the Valparaíso region, invites you to explore its many trails and uncover the mysteries of the moai. Discover mysterious caves with rock art, immerse yourself in the Rapanui culture, and relax on the beautiful beaches of Anakena and Ovahe. Explore the underwater paradise surrounding the Motu islets

Villarrica National Park

Villarrica National Park

Located 8 km from Pucón, in the Araucanía region, amidst the Villarrica Volcano, you'll find Villarrica National Park. Covering 63,000 hectares, this park stretches across Curarrehue, Panguipulli, Pucón, and Villarrica, offering stunning views of the Lanín, Quetrupillán, and Villarrica volcanoes. The Villarrica volcano itself is particularly popular, featuring a ski center. You can also explore various trails and enjoy the many activities available at Caburgua, Calafquén, and Villarrica Lakes.

Vicente Perez Rosales National Park

Vicente Perez Rosales National Park

Located 64 km from Puerto Varas in the Los Lagos region, Vicente Pérez Rosales National Park boasts lush evergreen forests filled with canelos, pitras, and coligues, home to pudúes and pumas. Key attractions include Osorno Volcano, Todos Los Santos Lake, the Puntiagudo, Tronador, and Cayutué valleys, the Petrohué Falls, and numerous trails leading to stunning viewpoints.

Torres Del Paine National Park

Torres Del Paine National Park

Located 112 km from Puerto Natales, in the Magallanes region, Torres del Paine National Park is one of the most famous national parks worldwide and is considered one of the most beautiful places on Earth, not for nothing it's a World Biosphere Reserve. Its valleys, mountains, rivers, lakes, and glaciers create a unique landscape, making it a paradise for trekking and hiking enthusiasts. With three circuits of varying difficulty, you can explore the Grey Glacier, the base of the Towers, and the French Valley.
